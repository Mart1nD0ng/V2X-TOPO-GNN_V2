# Envelope Characterization — Results (P0 + D1 + D4)

Authoritative record for the envelope-paper deliverables. Currency: quenched (train Q=11 / eval
Q=21) on `configs/paper_environment_v1.yaml`. Supersedes the single-seed "18-cell advantage region"
headline in earlier docs (which is hereby **retracted** — see §1). Generated by:
`run_advantage_map.py --seeds 42,7,123` (P0), `--profiles high_reliability_synthetic,very_hard_low_confidence`
+ `--node-count 1800` (D1), `eval_scale_transfer.py` (D4).

## 1. P0 — seed-banded advantage map (the honest re-count)

27 cells × 3 seeds (42, 7, 123), N=600, 120 steps. **Wall-clock = 52.8 min** (first time recorded).
Each seed re-draws the vehicle layout AND the xavier init together, so the spread is the honest
variance of the **label** (gap = bestH − F_gnn), not just init noise. An advantage label is **ROBUST**
only if `gap_mean − 2σ > 0.010` AND `headroom_mean − 2σ > 0.020`.

`result/advantage_map/advantage_map.json`

| region | cells | verdict | gap (mean ± σ) | note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **density 100** (sparse, area 6 km²) | 9/9 | **GNN_ADVANTAGE — ROBUST** | +0.080 … +0.106 ± ~0.006 | lower-2σ bound far above 0.010 |
| **density 200, coupling 10/20 dB** | 6 | **GNN_ADVANTAGE — FRAGILE** | +0.013 … +0.017 ± ~0.003–0.004 | positive mean, but gap−2σ < 0.010 |
| **density 200, coupling 0 dB** | 3 | **HEURISTIC_PARITY** (demoted) | +0.009 … +0.010 ± ~0.003 | 3-seed mean now sub-threshold |
| **density 300** (dense, area 2 km²) | 9/9 | **FLOOR_LIMITED** (robust) | +0.001 … +0.004 ± ~0.0005–0.001 | graceful parity at the floor |

**σ_seed (gap):** ~0.006 at d100, ~0.003–0.004 at d200, ~0.0005–0.001 at d300.

**RETRACTION.** The earlier headline "18/27 GNN_ADVANTAGE" was a **single-seed (seed 42)**
classification. Seed 42 was a *favorable draw at the d200 boundary*: the 3-seed means there are
systematically lower, dropping 3 cells below the advantage threshold and leaving the other 6
advantage-on-the-mean but **not robust** at 2σ. The honest statement is:

> The **robustly** GNN-advantaged region is the **9 sparse-density (100 veh/km²) cells** (gap +0.08…+0.11,
> ~13–18× σ_seed). The density-200 boundary is **marginal/fragile** (6 cells positive-but-not-robust,
> 3 cells at parity). Density-300 is **robustly floor-limited** (graceful parity). The GNN advantage
> is real and large where the candidate links sit on the reliability cliff (sparse), and vanishes
> into the protocol floor as density saturates the graph.

This σ_seed (~0.003–0.006) is the threshold the D3 mixture-generalist retention bar must clear; a
d200 "advantage retention" claim is not licensed without it.

## 2. D1b — confidence-axis lattice extension (ic ∈ [0.25, 0.90])

The two ic-extreme profiles the floor table already defines, scored on floor + heuristics + a short
GNN (1 seed, free of new floor-table work — floor is keyed by profile).
`result/advantage_map_ic_extremes/advantage_map.json`

| profile | protocol floor (deg 4) | F ≤ 0.01 feasible? | advantage structure |
|---|---|---|---|
| `high_reliability_synthetic` (ic 0.90) | **0.0048** | **YES** — floor below target | GNN advantage persists at d100 (headroom +0.11…+0.13) and d200 c10/c20; d300 floor-limited |
| `near_target_synthetic` (ic 0.65) | 0.064 | no (budget 4) | (P0) sparse advantage / dense floor-limited |
| `toy` (ic 0.50) | 0.064 | no (budget 4) | (P0) sparse advantage / dense floor-limited |
| `hard_low_confidence` (ic 0.40) | 0.064 | no (budget 4) | (P0) sparse advantage / dense floor-limited |
| `very_hard_low_confidence` (ic 0.25) | **0.9455** | **NO** — hopeless | all floor-limited; F ≈ 0.6 regardless of topology |

**Reading.** The **protocol floor is the binding feasibility constraint**, and it is governed by the
initial-confidence regime: high-reliability operation (F ≤ 0.01) only becomes feasible once ic reaches
~0.90, where the floor drops to 0.0048 — and *even there* the GNN advantage structure is preserved
(sparse cells win, dense cells hit the floor). At ic = 0.25 the consensus is degenerate (floor 0.945);
no topology helps. This widens the confidence axis from the earlier ic ∈ [0.40, 0.65] to ic ∈ [0.25,
0.90] and turns "where does high-reliability live?" into a floor-anchored answer.

*Footnote (ic 0.25):* headroom is slightly negative there (bestH ≈ 0.63 < ring-floor 0.945). The
"floor" is a perfect-link **ring** reference at degree 4; at pathological ic the realized candidate
graph can numerically beat that specific construction. The honest reading is "F ≤ 0.01 infeasible, no
useful advantage", not a literal sub-floor achievement.

## 3. D1c — area-confound spot check (defuses the density-vs-mapsize objection)

In the main grid, density co-varies with area at fixed N=600 (area = N/density), so "density-300 is
floor-limited" could mean "small-area / saturated candidate graph". Controlled test: re-run
density-300 at **N=1800** so area = 6 km² — the SAME area as the density-100 / N=600 advantage cells.
`result/advantage_map_area_control/advantage_map.json`

| cell | area | headroom | gap | class |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| d300 / hard / c20, **N=1800** | 6 km² | +0.0051 | +0.0046 | FLOOR_LIMITED |
| d300 / hard / c20, N=600 (P0 ref) | 2 km² | +0.0051 | +0.0041 | FLOOR_LIMITED |
| d100 / hard / c20, N=600 (P0 ref) | 6 km² | +0.1791 | +0.1062 | GNN_ADVANTAGE |

**Reading.** Holding area fixed at 6 km², density-300 stays floor-limited (headroom unchanged at
+0.0051) while density-100 is strongly advantaged. So the operative variable is **density**
(vehicles/km² → candidate-graph saturation), **not** map size / area. The confound is real in the
grid's labelling but the controlled check shows it does not drive the verdict: "density-300 is
floor-limited" is honest. (Single seed at N=1800; a fuller area sweep is deferred.)

## 4b. D3 — mixture generalist + leave-one-density-out (the new training result)

`result/mixture_generalization/envelope_generalization.json`, fig `result/envelope_figures/generalization.png`.
ONE unconditioned model, mixture-trained across the (density × coupling × profile) envelope via the
D3-fix-1 per-env evaluator coupling (parity-tested, `tests/analysis/test_env_physical_config_parity.py`).
Currency quenched eval Q=21, N=600, 40 round-robin steps/cell. Retention = gap_model / gap_expert,
scored only where the P0 expert gap is materially positive (>0.02); expert = the P0 screening GNN
(labelled screening-grade, NOT a claim).

**Three findings, in order of strength:**

1. **Held-out-density generalization is strong (leave-one-density-out).** Train on two densities,
   evaluate the THIRD, unseen density:
   | held-out density | retention vs per-cell expert | held-out gap_model |
   |---|---|---|
   | **d100** (robust-advantage region) | **0.993** (n=9) | +0.0947 (≈ expert +0.095) |
   | **d200** (marginal boundary) | **0.850** (n=5) | +0.0160 |
   | d300 (floor-limited) | n/a (no material-gap cells) | +0.0021 |
   The learned edge-scoring function transfers across density: a model that never saw density-100
   matches the per-cell d100 expert to within 1%.

2. **Off-grid interpolation works.** The all-cells mixture evaluated at off-grid operating points it
   NEVER trained on (density {150, 250} × coupling {5, 15} dB): **12/12 cells beat heuristics** —
   d150 gap +0.0172, d250 gap +0.0073. The advantage interpolates smoothly into the envelope interior.

3. **Negative transfer in the naive 3-density joint mixture (the honest caveat).** The all-cells
   mixture `M_all` UNDER-fits each density relative to the 2-density LOCO models — consistently:
   | density | M_all retention (trained ON it) | LOCO retention (trained WITHOUT it) | Δ |
   |---|---|---|---|
   | d100 | 0.338 (F_model 0.185) | 0.993 (F_model 0.123) | **LOCO +0.66** |
   | d200 | 0.688 (F_model 0.071) | 0.850 (F_model 0.068) | **LOCO +0.16** |
   Removing a density from training IMPROVES test performance on it — a gradient-interference /
   negative-transfer effect across the heterogeneous density axis (d100 high-failure cells vs
   d300 floor cells pull the shared scorer in conflicting directions). At matched per-cell steps the
   3-density round-robin under-optimizes the high-value d100 region.

**Honest verdict (updated by T1 below).** The generalization CAPACITY is confirmed (LOCO 0.85–0.99;
off-grid 12/12); the **naive** all-cells mixture did not realize it. T1 shows WHY and FIXES it: the
"negative transfer" was a **round-robin ordering artifact**, not a fundamental obstacle — a balanced
per-step multi-density gradient recovers the LOCO ceiling. See §4c.

The pre-registered FiLM-conditioned arm is **deferred** (S3 null precedent + critique "cut if tight").

## 4c. T1 — gradient-governed mixture (the negative transfer is resolved AND explained)

`result/envelope_governed_{gradnorm,pcgrad}_v1/`, fig `result/envelope_figures/governed_retention.png`.
Generic per-density gradient governance (`generalization_common.py::{density_gradient_conflict,
train_model_governed}`, reusing `pcgrad_project` / `compute_task_gradient_vectors` /
`GradNormBalancer`), driver `run_envelope_governed.py`. Each governed step draws one env per density,
computes one gradient vector per density, and merges them (PCGrad projection or GradNorm reweighting);
per-cell exposure matched to the naive M_all (40/cell).

**Step 0 — GO/NO-GO diagnostic (the pre-registered gate, and it redirected the fix).** Per-density
gradient norms/cosines at the shared init: norms d100=802, **d200=1709** (2.1×), d300=959; cosines all
**POSITIVE** (d100·d200 **0.822**, d100·d300 0.217, d200·d300 0.239). Reading: **no directional
conflict** (so PCGrad's projection is a no-op), but a **magnitude imbalance** — the under-fit d100
region has the SMALLEST gradient norm and is starved in the mix. (The 0.822 d100·d200 alignment also
explains §4b's surprise: training on d200 moves 82% in d100's direction, hence the strong LOCO.)

**Step 1–2 — in-grid retention by density (governed vs naive vs LOCO ceiling):**
| density | naive round-robin | PCGrad (= balanced batch) | GradNorm | LOCO ceiling |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| d100 | 0.338 | **0.988** | **1.005** | 0.993 |
| d200 | 0.688 | **0.849** | **0.907** | 0.850 |
d100 F_model: naive 0.185 → GradNorm **0.122** (= the per-cell expert ~0.123). Off-grid interpolation
mean gap also improves: naive +0.0122 → GradNorm **+0.0197** (12/12 positive in all arms).

**Mechanism (honest, sharper than the D3 caveat).** PCGrad with all-positive cosines performs NO
projection — it is just the **mean of the three per-density gradients each step**, i.e. a balanced
multi-density mini-batch. That alone lifts d100 retention 0.34 → 0.99. So the D3 "negative transfer"
was an artifact of **naive round-robin ordering** (sequential single-cell updates let the optimizer
drift toward whichever density it last saw); a balanced per-step gradient removes it. **GradNorm adds
a small further edge** (d100 1.005, d200 0.907, both ≥ ceiling) by also fixing the d100 magnitude
starvation the diagnostic flagged — consistent with the diagnostic, not coincidence.

**Claim licensed now (the strongest version).** *"ONE gradient-governed mixture policy spans the
measured advantage envelope — matching per-cell experts in-grid (retention ≈1.00 d100, 0.91 d200,
F_model = the expert's), generalizing across held-out density (LOCO 0.85–0.99), and interpolating
off-grid (12/12, +0.020 gap)."* The D3 negative transfer is no longer a caveat — it is explained
(round-robin artifact) and fixed (balanced batching + GradNorm), which is a cleaner methodological
contribution than either "one mixture works" or "naive mixing fails" alone.

## 5. What this licenses (and what it does not)

- **Licensed now:** a seed-banded **screening envelope** (robust-advantage = 9 sparse cells; fragile
  boundary; floor-limited dense region), a **floor-anchored feasibility map** across ic ∈ [0.25, 0.90],
  the **density-not-area** attribution, a **precise scale-transfer-regret** number, a **domain-aware
  gating** demonstrator (D2: estimated density+SINR → policy, 15/15 frames in predicted band), and
  **held-out-density generalization + off-grid interpolation** (D3 LOCO 0.85–0.99; 12/12 off-grid),
  and (T1) a **gradient-governed mixture** that matches per-cell experts in-grid (retention ≈1.00 d100,
  0.91 d200) — the D3 negative transfer is resolved (round-robin artifact) and explained.
- **Caveats (state in-line every time):** single Manhattan snapshot, degree budget 4, no shadow/churn
  in the map, density = density at fixed N=600 (area-scaled, but D1c shows density is the driver),
  3 seeds (d100/d200/d300) / 1 seed (ic-extremes, area-check). Call it a **screening envelope**, not
  certification-grade.
- **Not licensed:** "18-cell advantage region" (retracted), "domain adaptation capability"
  (unsupported — see `DEPLOYMENT_ROADMAP`/DA-framing), any d200 retention claim without the σ_seed bar.

## 6. T3 — C3 emission-grounded stabilization (replicated, isolated, mechanism-explained)

`result/{emission_2x2_isolation,fine_stage_*,c3_repl_*,emission_probe_collapse_regime}`, figs
`result/envelope_figures/c3_{mechanism,probe_divergence}.png`. Frozen streaming spec (shadow 4 dB/8 s,
churn 2, dt 2), legacy `production_training_v1` physics (the regime where pure-memory recurrence
collapses; the paper-env physics does NOT collapse — a separate stability data point in
`result/emission_probe_paperenv`).

**T3b — 2×2 isolation (emission × graph-coupling), d100/hard/c20.** σ_init (init variance; collapse
metric):
| arm | emission | graph | recurrence | σ_init | worst F |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| full | ✗ | ✓ | ✓ | 0.2395 | 0.998 |
| no_graph | ✗ | ✗ | ✓ | 0.1658 | 0.975 |
| filter | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | **0.0061** | 0.585 |
| no_graph_filter | ✓ | ✗ | ✓ | **0.0072** | 0.603 |
| filter_nomem | ✓ | reset | ✗ | **0.0080** | 0.596 |
**Emission is the load-bearing factor**: σ_init drops ~30–39× iff emission is on, INDEPENDENT of graph
coupling (filter ≈ no_graph_filter, paired p=0.86) and even of recurrence (filter_nomem stable). The
graph coupling and the recurrent carry are NOT what stabilizes.

**T3a — replication (collapse+rescue is regime-specific, the honest scope).** full→filter σ_init:
d100/hard/c20 **39.2×** (worst 0.998→0.585), d100/toy/c20 **27.5×** (0.945→0.579), d100/hard/c0 3.1×
(0.532→0.500), d200/hard/c20 0.4× (no collapse: full already σ_init 0.0024). The pure-memory collapse —
and the emission rescue — is concentrated at **sparse density + high interference (the GNN-advantage
region)**; where there is no collapse (dense/floor-limited) emission is neutral (harmless). So emission
is a **targeted stabilizer for the hard operating points**, not a universal tweak. The temporal mean-F
null also replicates (full F_mean ≥ static — memory does not improve accuracy, only stability).

**T3c — mechanism (probe, collapse init=7, full vs filter).** WITHOUT emission the recurrent gate-input
scale `joined_std` GROWS monotonically 0.35→11.8 (~34×) while the graph-GRU gate gradient VANISHES to 0
— the model is stuck in the collapsed init (final F 0.96). WITH emission the calibrated [0,1]
observation keeps `joined_std` bounded (~2) so the gradient stays alive and the model escapes (F 0.57).
**Mechanism: the emission bounds the recurrent input distribution → keeps gradients flowing → escapes
the collapse.** This is the *how* under the 2×2's *what*.

## 7. T2 — C2 advantage hardening (5-seed bands · MC ground truth)

`result/{advantage_map,advantage_montecarlo,fine_stage_*}`.

**T2a — 5-seed map** (seeds 42,7,123,5,11; 88.8 min): 14 GNN_ADVANTAGE / 4 PARITY / 9 FLOOR_LIMITED.
Confirms the P0 3-seed verdict and tightens it: the **9 sparse d100 cells stay robust advantage**; the
d200 boundary settles to **5 fragile-advantage + 4 parity** (all c0 cells + near/c10 are parity under 5
seeds). The robust core is unchanged; the boundary is marginal as expected.

**T2b — supplementary fine cells.** Streaming paired-statistics on d100/toy/c20 and d200/hard/c20
(beyond the single d100/hard/c20): the temporal mean-F null and the emission stabilization both hold
(see §6); GNN beats fair stream heuristics on the advantage cells.

**T2c — Monte-Carlo ground-truth band** (1000 trials, 3 d100 cells): the GNN-beats-heuristic gap
**SURVIVES MC on 3/3 cells** — gap_quenched +0.099…+0.110 → gap_MC +0.067…+0.088 (shrinks slightly,
stays robustly positive). The absolute F is quenched-optimistic (e.g. GNN F 0.135→0.370 under MC) but
the **relative advantage is not a surrogate artifact** — it holds under direct simulation. This is the
ground-truth check the envelope claim needed.
